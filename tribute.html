Saint Mother Teresa

A mother to motherless and a beacon of hope for the hopeless

Here is a time line of Mother Teresa's life:

  • 1910 - Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu is born in Skopje, Albania

  • 1928 - Agnes joins the Sisters of Loreto and takes the name Teresa.

  • 1929 - Teresa travels to India to begin her novitiate.

  • 1937 - Teresa is professed as a nun and begins teaching at St. Mary's School in Kolkata.

  • 1946 - Teresa receives a calling to leave her convent and work with the poor in the slums of Kolkata.

  • 1950 - Teresa founds the Missionaries of Charity, a religious congregation dedicated to helping the poor and sick.

  • 1952 - Teresa opens the first home for the dying in Kolkata

  • 1965 - Teresa receives the Nobel Peace Prize for her work with the poor

  • 1985 - Teresa's health begins to decline

  • 1997 - Mother Teresa dies in Kolkata at the age of 87.

"Mother Teresa's life was a long and eventful one. She dedicated her life to helping the poor and sick, and she made a real difference in the world. She will be remembered for her compassion, her commitment to helping others, and her faith."
